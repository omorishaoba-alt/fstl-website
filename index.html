Welcome to FSTL OS v1.0

First Standard Title Limited (FSTL) brings 20 years of expertise in verification, title insurance, and trust infrastructure into a fully digital operating system. FSTL OS transforms real-world assets into bankable, investable, and inheritable capital for individuals, families, and institutions.

Mission: Elevate people out of poverty by turning assets into verifiable collateral.

Vision: To standardize asset verification and capital creation globally.

Retail Users

As an individual, FSTL OS allows you to:

  • Verify your assets quickly and securely
  • Buy title insurance and mortgage insurance
  • Use verified assets as clean collateral for loans
  • Transform your assets into investable capital

Flat rate: 50,000 NGN

All steps are simple, digital, and verifiable. Your collateral is protected even in inheritance or transfer.

Institutional Users

Institutions and government bodies can use FSTL OS to:

  • Verify and standardize multiple asset classes
  • Transform verified assets into ABS, MBS, corporate bonds, municipal bonds, and other structured instruments
  • Integrate into internal risk, compliance, and investment workflows

Fee: 5% of asset value

How FSTL OS Works

The flow of FSTL OS:

  1. Input: Raw assets (land, property, estate, or other verifiable assets)
  2. Transformation: Verification, title insurance, mortgage insurance, and structured collateralization
  3. Output: Investable, bankable, inheritable, and liquid capital

This digital OS ensures assets retain value, remain verifiable, and can generate capital for generations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How is FSTL OS different from GIS or C of O?

A: GIS only maps, C of O only certifies land ownership. FSTL OS verifies, insures, and transforms assets into real capital.

Q: Can I use FSTL OS if I’m not an institution?

A: Yes, individuals can use agents or act directly to transform assets into bankable collateral.

Q: What makes FSTL OS safe and credible?

A: All verification, insurance, and capital structures follow legal standards, with records backed digitally and auditable by financial institutions.
