Why FSTL Is an Operating System (OS)

FSTL is not a bank, not a land registry, and not a government ministry. FSTL is an Operating System for property, verification, trust, and capital formation.

Just as:
  • Windows runs computers
  • Android runs smartphones
  • iOS runs Apple devices
FSTL runs property, verification, and collateralisation.

Nigeria has assets but lacks a unified system to convert those assets into clean, trusted, bank-acceptable capital. FSTL fills that gap.

FSTL Core Logic:

INPUTS → Land, titles, documents, parties
TRANSFORMATION → Verification, standardisation, risk reduction
OUTPUTS → Bank-acceptable collateral, capital readiness

Retail Users (Individuals)

Many Nigerians own land or property but cannot use it to access capital. Banks do not reject people — they reject uncertainty.

Retail Verification — Flat Rate

₦50,000 (one-time)
Covers verification, transaction validation, and FSTL confirmation reference.

After FSTL verification, individuals can:

  • Buy Title Insurance
  • Apply for loans using verified collateral
  • Buy Mortgage Insurance to protect all parties

FSTL does not give loans. FSTL makes loans possible.

Agents (Facilitators)

Agents support individuals who are not digitally confident. They reduce friction — not extract value.

Agent Commission:
10% per completed transaction

Agents do not set prices, alter terms, or make promises on behalf of FSTL.

Investment Representatives (FSTL Marketers)

Investment Representatives are authorised to directly represent FSTL in the market — engaging investors, developers, institutions, and government entities.

Commission:
20% per completed transaction

Privileges:

  • Permission to use FSTL-branded business cards
  • Recognised as “Investment Representative – First Standard Title Limited”

Representation rights are permission-based and revocable to protect trust.

Institutions (Banks, Investors, Government)

Institutions do not lack money. They lack trusted, standardised, investment-grade assets.

Using FSTL-verified assets, institutions can structure:
  • Asset-Backed Securities (ABS)
  • Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS)
  • Corporate Bonds
  • Municipal Bonds

Institutional Rate: 5% of asset value
